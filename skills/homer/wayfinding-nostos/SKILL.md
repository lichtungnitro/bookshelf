# Wayfinding (Nostos)

> *Nostos* is the return: not mere travel, but **coming back to oneself and one’s place** after what dismembers attention. Odysseus is kept from Ithaca by storms and by choices; the Lotus makes men forget; Circe offers a pause that can become a life. The skill is to **keep the north star named** while honestly listing what can pull you off course.

## When to use

- A finish line exists but the route does not (or it keeps changing)
- You or the team have "forgotten Ithaca" (Lotus pattern)
- You must choose between bad options (Scylla and Charybdis pattern)
- You need kill criteria: how you will know you are *home*, not merely busy

## Executable workflow

### 1. Name Ithaca (one paragraph)

Write what **done** means in lived terms: who is safe, what is restored, what obligation is discharged. If you cannot say it plainly, you are not ready to wayfind—only to wander.

### 2. Inventory trials (honest)

List **external** obstacles (resources, dependencies, politics) and **internal** ones (fear, pride, fatigue). Odysseus loses crew to hunger and disobedience, not only to Poseidon. Name yours without moral theater.

### 3. Tag derailment patterns

| Pattern | Odyssey anchor | Question |
|--------|----------------|----------|
| Lotus | Lotus-eaters | What would make us **forget the goal** if we tasted it daily? |
| Siren | Sirens | What sounds **wise** but steers the ship onto rocks? |
| Underworld | Nekyia | What dead assumption must we **consult** before moving? |
| Double bind | Scylla / Charybdis | What loss is **certain** if we optimize for zero risk? |

### 5. Cadence

Set a review that only asks: **Are we closer to Ithaca, or only busier?** If busier, prune before adding.

## Tone

Calm, unsentimental, and willing to name self-inflicted delay. The poem punishes both cruelty and foolishness; this skill punishes vague goals and unnamed temptations.
