# Reference — Under the sun

## Primary texts (ESV)

- Ecclesiastes 1:2–3 — vanity, gain under the sun
- Ecclesiastes 2:1–11 — experiment with pleasure, wisdom, works
- Ecclesiastes 3:1–8 — season for every matter
- Ecclesiastes 5:10–20 — wealth, gift, enjoyment
- Ecclesiastes 9:7–10 — eat bread with joy, work with might
- Ecclesiastes 12:13–14 — fear God, keep commandments, judgment

## Misuse guards

- **Not an excuse for sloth**: the Teacher commends diligent work in its season; he attacks *delusion about outcomes*.
- **Not universal cynicism**: "meaningless" (vanity/vapor) targets **misplaced ultimacy**, not craft or love.
- **Not anti-ambition**: ambition under bounded honesty differs from ambition as identity.

## Contrast with Stoicism / Buddhism in brief

- Qoheleth is **historical and liturgical** in setting; the closure is **theocentric** (fear God, commandments). Secular users can still apply **bounded optimization** and **seasonality** without importing doctrine.
