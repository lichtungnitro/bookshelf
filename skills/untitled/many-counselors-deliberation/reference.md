# Reference — Many counselors

## Primary texts (ESV)

- Proverbs 11:14 — safety in abundance of counselors
- Proverbs 15:22 — many advisers, plans succeed
- Proverbs 24:6 — war by wise guidance, victory in many advisers
- Proverbs 18:17 — first speech vs cross-examination
- Proverbs 27:6 — faithful wounds vs enemy kisses
- Proverbs 14:29 — slow to anger, great understanding
- Proverbs 16:9 — heart plans way; LORD establishes steps
- Proverbs 27:1 — do not boast about tomorrow

## Anti-patterns

- **Counsel shopping**: asking until someone agrees
- **Seniority veto**: rank replacing argument
- **False diversity**: same assumptions, different job titles
- **Premature closure**: "we already decided" before second speech

## Relation to other skills

- Differs from Socratic dialectic: emphasis on *multiple embodied perspectives* and *ordered testimony*, not only logical elenchus.
- Complements post-mortems: use *before* the failure when possible.
