# Reference — Prophetic systems diagnosis

## Textual anchors (ESV)

- Jeremiah 2:13 — broken cisterns vs fountain of living water
- Amos 5:10–15 — justice at the gate; hate evil, love good
- Isaiah 1:16–17 — wash yourselves; seek justice, correct oppression
- Ezekiel 34:2–4 — shepherds feeding themselves vs sheep
- Micah 6:8 — do justice, love kindness, walk humbly

## Secular use

- **Idol** → **unquestioned optimization target**
- **Repentance** → **incentive and metric change**
- **Prophet** → **role authorized to name what power avoids** (often staff engineer, SRE, support lead, union rep)

## Boundaries

- This skill diagnoses **structure**; it does not license **personal contempt**.
- If harm involves abuse or illegality, **escalate through proper channels** — analysis is not substitute for HR, legal, or safety processes.

## Contrasts

- **Socratic dialectic**: tests claims; prophetic diagnosis tests **what the organization cannot afford to lose face about**.
- **Many counselors**: plural voices; prophetic work often begins **before** those voices are allowed to speak freely.
