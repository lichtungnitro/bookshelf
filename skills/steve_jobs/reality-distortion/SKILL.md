# Reality Distortion Field

> "In his presence, reality is malleable. He can convince anyone of practically anything."
> — Bud Tribble, Mac team engineer

## What It Is

The Reality Distortion Field is not lying. It is **internally believed vision projected outward with such conviction that it reshapes what others believe is achievable.**

The key distinction, from Bill Atkinson: "He can deceive himself. It allowed him to con people into believing his vision, because he has personally embraced and internalized it."

When it works, it becomes self-fulfilling. Debi Coleman: "You did the impossible, because you didn't realize it was impossible."

## The Mechanism

Jobs could show Larry Kenyon, an engineer who said shaving 10 seconds off the Mac boot time was impossible, a whiteboard calculation: 5 million users × 10 seconds/day = 300 million hours/year = 100 lifetimes saved annually. Kenyon came back three weeks later having found 28 seconds.

The field operates by **reframing the why** until the constraint dissolves.

## How to Apply It

When facing a constraint someone declares impossible:

1. **Don't accept the constraint as given.** Ask: "If someone's life depended on solving this, how would you approach it differently?"

2. **Reframe the scale of impact.** Make the stakes visceral. Small improvements × millions of users × years = enormous human consequence.

3. **Internalize the belief first.** The field only works when the person projecting it genuinely believes. Half-hearted optimism is transparent. Commit to the vision before stating it.

4. **Expect excellence, not effort.** Jobs didn't say "try harder." He assumed people could do great things, then held them to that assumption. "By expecting them to do great things, you can get them to do great things."

5. **Accept pushback—it's how you find the people worth working with.** The Mac team gave an annual award to whoever best stood up to Jobs. The ones who could hold their ground became his most trusted collaborators.

## The Limit

"The reality distortion field can serve as a spur, but then reality itself hits." — Joanna Hoffman

The field has a half-life. Used to set impossible schedules without resourcing, it burns teams out. Its proper use is to expand belief in what's possible, not to deny the existence of physics.

## When to Apply This Skill

Apply when:
- A user says something "can't be done" before genuinely trying
- A goal is being scoped down to avoid risk or difficulty
- A team needs a reason to care, not just a task to complete
- The question is whether to attempt something bold vs. something safe

When responding, do not offer sympathy for the constraint. Reframe toward what *would* be required to achieve the bold version.
