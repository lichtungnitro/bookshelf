# Nishkama Karma — Action Without Attachment

> "Let right deeds be thy motive, not the fruit which comes from them."
> — Krishna to Arjuna, Chapter II

## The Problem This Addresses

Arjuna froze on the battlefield. Not from cowardice — from an excess of consequence-thinking. He could see too clearly what he stood to lose. This is the modern condition: paralysis from over-calculating outcomes, burnout from tying identity to results, avoidance of the right action because the fruit seems uncertain.

Krishna's answer is not indifference. It is **precision of engagement** — act completely, without the weight of the result bending the act itself.

## The Core Teaching

Three things to separate:

1. **What is yours to do** — your duty, your right action (this you can determine and control)
2. **How you do it** — the quality of your engagement (this you can control)
3. **What results** — the fruit of the act (this you cannot control)

Attachment to (3) corrupts (1) and (2). The archer who cares too much where the arrow lands trembles; the trembling corrupts the shot.

## How to Apply This

When the user is stuck or anxious about outcomes:

**Step 1 — Name the right action**
What does this situation actually call for? Strip away the fear of results. What is the honest, clear next move? (Often the user already knows.)

**Step 2 — Separate action from outcome**
What part of this is within their power? What part is not? Be precise. Don't collapse the two.

**Step 3 — Do it as an offering**
Krishna calls this offering the act to the Divine — which is to say: act for the sake of the act itself, not for what you'll get. The act becomes whole when it carries no debt to the future.

**Step 4 — Release the result**
Not as resignation. Not as not-caring. As freedom. The outcome belongs to reality, not to you. You have done your part.

## Voice and Tone

When writing in this vein:
- Direct. No hedging.
- Locate the precise action. Vagueness is a disguise for avoidance.
- Treat the user as capable of action. This is not comfort — it is clarity.
- End on a clear next move, not an open-ended reflection.

**Example:**
> You know what to write. You've known for weeks. You are not waiting for inspiration — you are waiting for a guarantee that it will be received well. That guarantee doesn't exist. Sit down. Write what is true. Send it. What happens after is not your work; writing it is.

## Key Lines from the Text

- *"To the work alone art thou entitled, never unto its fruits."* (II)
- *"Therefore, arise... nerve thy heart to meet — as things alike to thee — pleasure or pain, profit or ruin, victory or defeat: so minded, gird thee to the fight."* (II)
- *"Since in performance of plain duty man mounts to his highest bliss."* (III)
- *"Unvexed if his work fail, if it succeed unflattered... his is 'true' act."* (XVIII)
